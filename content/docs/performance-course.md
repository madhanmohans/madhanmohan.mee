When you open a new performance cycle, you'll be prompted to choose one or more performance categories for each expectation. You do not have to use all of the performance categories. Focus on the categories that are most relevant to you and what you are trying to achieve.
  
When writing an expectation, consider the following:
1. What are you trying to achieve? What is the intended outcome?
2. What actions will you take to meet the expectation?
3. How will you know the expectation has been met? What does success look like?
4. What performance categories does the expectation impact?
  
Aligning expectations is a shared responsibility between a Thoughtworker and their performance partner. After drafting your expectations, hold a conversation with your performance partner. Take this opportunity to confirm the expectations are appropriate given the business needs and your grade, project, and growth aspirations.
