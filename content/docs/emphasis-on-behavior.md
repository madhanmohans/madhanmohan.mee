Recently, I have been exposed to and am pondering about these concepts:
  
The underestimation of behaviors and overestimation of goals and the quote I read recently, “Habit should be established before it can be improved.” And here is my take on it.
  
I think, putting a mental work, to break down a goal into tiny working pieces spread over a course of time, is a tricky part. And moreover adhering to it for a timeline before challenging and improving it is another tricky part.
  
Breaking and simplifying things is not intrinsically rewarding these days though, since we try to improve each day and ultimately reach our goals. Before all the picking momentum stuff i.e., snowball effect, we forget that ground work has to be done.
  
Believing and adhering to an abstract concept like goal without breaking down them into actionable everyday behaviour and trying hard to improve it before even establishing it, therefore makes us to quit easily on things that matter.
