## Key Points:
- [0:40] Testosterone is the male sex hormone that plays a crucial role in various bodily functions, including libido, muscle mass, bone density, and metabolism.
- [1:19] Testosterone levels naturally decline with age, with a gradual decrease of 1-2% per year, typically becoming noticeable in men from their 50s and beyond.
- [1:38] However, low testosterone can also be caused by underlying diseases or medical problems, such as dietary factors, being overweight or obese, excessive stress, lack of sleep, and reduced sexual activity.
- [2:09] Consuming too much soy, bisphenol A (found in plastics), and alcohol can contribute to lower testosterone levels.
- [2:22] Excess body fat can lead to the conversion of testosterone into estrogen, further reducing testosterone production.
- [2:42] Chronic stress and lack of sleep can indirectly decrease testosterone levels by increasing cortisol production.
- [3:04] Infrequent sexual activity can create a downward spiral, leading to lower libido and further reductions in testosterone.
- [3:53] Various medical conditions, such as chronic illnesses, delayed puberty, testicular damage or cancer, and genetic disorders, can also cause low testosterone.
- [4:11] Certain treatments, including chemotherapy, radiation therapy, hormone therapy, and corticosteroid drugs, can contribute to decreased testosterone levels.
