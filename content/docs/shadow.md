If one fears a thing, it might be the case it is the door
  
In the process of understanding oneself, facing one's fear is the door. It's not a piece of typical advice that I'm trying to reiterate. It's the internal fear that hovers around in your mind. It's the opposite of what you know about yourself. The fear of being exposed to what you really are. We fear that. We are deeply flawed and we know that more than anyone else.
  
Our moral principles might not be complete because of knowing only the known. To be consistent with what we think or what we wish we ought to be, we suppress the unknown, deep inside. We suppress, to not expose our own vulnerabilities.
  
Be it any conversation, the one that finds its way towards the oppressed thoughts and being, won't fail to surprise you. It's simple. Because it deals with the truth in its totality. There is no fabricating or twisting.
  
One naturally fears the unknown to some extent, it's evolutionary, that's why our ancestors haven't risked their lives all the time, to deal with the unknown all the time, yet they also were not done with the known(it was a slow process though). Yet, psychologically speaking, there are thoughts every day, thoughts have roots, by that I say they mean something. It is rational, so it carries meaning.
Neglecting thoughts only because it leads to the unknown parts or rather unwilling parts of oneself is irrational. Those might be alarms. Alarms are to attended. They might be indirect or abstract, but they have their own place similar to the morally right thoughts. Living in bliss and therefore the known will never be the way to fully understand oneself. Rationally following the nature of thoughts to their core, genuinely acknowledging them, before eliminating them on an ethical basis, will help one to understand thyself.
  
Anyway, that ethical filter will be on the action as the thought grows.
